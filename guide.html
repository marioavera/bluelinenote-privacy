BlueLineNote User Guide

From the first crash report to a finished V.E.R.A. reconstruction.

Overview

BlueLineNote keeps everything on your device. You build a report, capture the scene, and export a PDF — nothing is uploaded to anyone. This guide walks the Crash workflow end to end, with the most detail on V.E.R.A., the built-in crash reconstruction tool.

The flow is simple: Build the report → Open V.E.R.A. → Reconstruct the scene → Export the PDF. Each part below follows that order.

Part 1 · Start a crash report

  1. From the dashboard, start a new Crash report.
  2. Fill the top fields as you normally would: case / complaint number, date, and time.
  3. Work down the sections — Location, Vehicles & Pedestrians, narrative, and signatures. You can leave V.E.R.A. for last; it pulls vehicle data from the report automatically.

Everything autosaves as you go. You can close the report and return to it later without losing work.

Set the crash location

The Location section drives the aerial map in V.E.R.A., so set it before you reconstruct. You have three ways:

OptionUse it when…
Use Current LocationYou're standing at the scene. It reads GPS and fills the address automatically.
Drop Pin on MapYou already know the exact latitude/longitude and want to type them in.
Load Map from AddressYou're not at the scene — e.g. a complainant reported a cross street. Type the street or intersection in Street Address / Scene Reference, then tap this. The map loads there so you can drag to the exact spot in V.E.R.A.
You don't have to be perfectly accurate here. When you drag the aerial in V.E.R.A. to the real crash point and save, BlueLineNote updates this location to match — coordinates and address both. So a nearby reference is enough to get started.

Add vehicles & people

  1. Open Vehicles & Pedestrians. Vehicle 1 is already there; tap Add Another Vehicle for each additional unit.
  2. For each vehicle, fill what you have: vehicle type, plate, and damage notes.
  3. Add pedestrians the same way if any were involved.

The labels (Vehicle 1, Vehicle 2…) are what V.E.R.A. uses when it syncs the units onto the canvas, so they carry straight through.

Mark impact & damage

Each vehicle has an impact diagram — a top-down outline of the vehicle. Tap the points on the outline where the vehicle was struck or damaged. This records the points of impact for the report and gives you a quick visual the reconstruction can build on.

Part 2 · V.E.R.A. crash reconstruction

V.E.R.A. turns the crash into a clear, to-scale diagram over a real aerial/street map: vehicle positions, directions of travel, paths, and scene objects. The result embeds into your crash PDF and can be exported as a still image or an animated clip.

Open V.E.R.A. and the two modes

In the crash report, open the Reconstruction & Narrative group and tap Open Crash Reconstruction. The canvas opens with your scene.

The toolbar has two working modes — switch with the mode chips:

  • Build — add and arrange the scene: vehicles, paths, markers, scale, north.
  • Photo — work with the background map: move it, recenter it, or switch between the live street map and a plain diagram.

Save stays pinned at the bottom in both modes, one tap away.

The background map, Move Map & Recenter

If the report has a location, V.E.R.A. loads a live aerial/street map centered there. In Photo mode:

  • Move Map — drag the map under the fixed vehicle silhouettes until the real crash spot (e.g. the actual intersection) sits under your scene. Tap it again (Done) to lock it.
  • Recenter — snaps the map back to the location the report opened with. Use this if you drag too far and lose the crash site, then start panning again from a known point.
  • Diagram / Street Map — toggle between the live map and a plain road diagram when you don't want a photo background.
Why this matters: when you Move Map to the real crash location and save, the report's GPS and address update to that spot automatically. You reconstruct and fix the location in one motion.

Sync vehicles from the report

Tap Sync (Build mode) to pull the vehicles you entered in the crash report onto the canvas as silhouettes — correctly labeled. Do this after you've added your vehicles so you don't place them by hand.

Place vehicles & set direction of travel

  1. Drag each vehicle silhouette to where it ended up on the map.
  2. Rotate it so it faces its direction of travel — the silhouette's heading is what the narrative reads when it describes which way each unit was going.
  3. Use Snap On/Off to toggle grid snapping while you line vehicles up to lanes and edges.

Draw crash paths

A path shows how a vehicle traveled into (and through) the collision.

  1. Tap a vehicle on the canvas to select it.
  2. Tap Path to give it a crash path. The path is made of waypoints you can drag to trace the route; a path needs at least two.
  3. Adjust waypoints to match skid marks, point of impact, and final rest position.

Paths are also what drive playback and the animated GIF — see Save, PNG & GIF.

Add scene objects (markers)

Tap Add Marker (Build mode) to open the Add Object sheet. Objects are grouped into three categories so they're quick to scan:

  • Traffic Controls — Stop Sign, Yield, Traffic Light, Speed Limit, One Way, Do Not Enter, No Parking.
  • Warnings — Merge, Pedestrian Crossing, Construction Zone, Railroad Crossing, Dead End, Detour, School Crossing.
  • Scene Objects — Impact Point, Fixed Object, Pedestrian.

Each object appears as a tile with an icon and a name. Tap one to drop it on the canvas, then drag it into position. Add as many as you need.

How objects behave on the canvas
Each object is placed, moved, and removed independently of the vehicles. Put them where they actually were at the scene. They stay in canvas space, so they hold their position relative to the vehicles even when you move or zoom the background map.

Cover panels (hide what doesn't belong)

A live aerial sometimes shows things that aren't part of your scene — parked cars, bystanders' property, unrelated structures. Drop a cover panel over that content to hide it. You can resize, rotate, and remove panels (Remove Cover). Use them to keep the diagram focused on the crash.

Set the scale & north

  • Scale — opens Scale Reference, the real-world distance the scale bar represents (default 50 ft). Set it so distances on the diagram read true.
  • Scene Heading (compass) — indicate which way is true north when the streets don't run straight up/down on the canvas. This keeps directions in the narrative correct.

Generate a draft narrative

Once vehicles and paths are placed, tap AI Draft to generate a written reconstruction narrative from the scene — directions of travel, sequence, and outcome — which you can apply back into the crash report and edit. It runs on the device; nothing leaves the phone.

Save, PNG snapshot & GIF clip

ActionWhat it does
SaveSaves the reconstruction to the crash note. The scene image is embedded in the exported crash PDF.
PNGExports a high-resolution still (about 1200 px wide) of the diagram through the share sheet — crisp enough for a court exhibit. Saved on the device, named from the case number when one is set.
GIFRecords an animated clip of the vehicles moving along their paths — about 6 frames per second, up to ~400 px wide. Kept deliberately light so it stays small to share and plays anywhere. Needs at least one vehicle path. Saved on the device and shared through the share sheet.
PlayPlays the reconstruction on screen so you can review the sequence before recording. Pause and Restart are available during playback; adjust the speed with the seconds control.
No paths, no motion. The GIF and Play replay vehicle paths. If a vehicle has no path, it sits still in the clip — draw paths first for anything you want to see move.

Part 3 · Full walkthrough

A complete pass, using a common situation: a crash reported at an intersection you're not standing at.

  1. Start the crash report. Enter the case number, date, and time.
  2. Set a reference location. The complainant says it happened at E 33rd St & Greenmount Ave, Baltimore, MD 21218. Type that into Street Address / Scene Reference and tap Load Map from Address.
  3. Add the vehicles. Add each unit, with type, plate, and damage notes, and mark the points of impact on each vehicle's diagram.
  4. Open V.E.R.A. from Reconstruction & Narrative.
  5. Sync to pull your vehicles onto the canvas.
  6. Move Map (Photo mode) so the real intersection sits under your silhouettes. Dragged too far? Tap Recenter and try again. Tap Done to lock it.
  7. Place & aim each vehicle at its resting spot, rotated to its direction of travel.
  8. Draw paths for each moving unit — select the vehicle, tap Path, drag the waypoints.
  9. Add markers for signs, signals, and debris; drop cover panels over anything unrelated.
  10. Set Scale and north so distances and directions read true.
  11. Play to review, then AI Draft for a narrative, and Save.
  12. Export the crash PDF. Your reconstruction is embedded, and the location now reflects the actual crash spot you dragged to — not your reference. Optionally export a PNG or GIF too.

Troubleshooting

The map is blank in V.E.R.A.
No location is set yet. Go back to the report's Location section and use Use Current Location, Drop Pin, or Load Map from Address first.
I dragged the map too far and can't find the crash site.
Tap Recenter in Photo mode to jump back to where the map started, then pan again.
My vehicles aren't on the canvas.
Tap Sync in Build mode. Make sure you added the vehicles in the crash report first.
The GIF doesn't show vehicles moving.
Give each moving vehicle a Path. Vehicles without a path stay still during playback and in the clip.
The location on my report didn't change after I dragged the map.
The update happens when you Save the reconstruction. Save the scene, then check the Location section.
